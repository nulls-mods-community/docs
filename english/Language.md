# How to Write a Good Description

Some tips to make your mod more appealing to users who care about quality writing.

### Pay attention to formatting

Start sentences with a capital letter. Use quotation marks where needed. Put a space
after a comma, colon, or semicolon. Avoid double spaces between words.

Bad:
<div style="background: #FF00001F">

> this is my mod,and what are you &nbsp;gonna do.it adds the abracadabra skin to the game
</div>

Good:
<div style="background: #00FF001F">

> This is my mod, and what are you gonna do? It adds the &laquo;Abracadabra&raquo; skin to the game.
</div>

### Follow language norms

Don't forget punctuation. Try to spell words correctly. Write proper sentences.

Bad:
<div style="background: #FF00001F">

> I very much to love cow so this mod adds cow to game. Hope you will like.
</div>

Good:
<div style="background: #00FF001F">

> I love cows, so this mod adds **a cow** to the game. Hope you enjoy it!
</div>

### Think about the content

When writing a mod description, try to answer: "why would this mod be useful?",
"what does it change?" and "how does it affect the game?".

The more specific and the less filler — the better. Nobody wants to install a mystery box.

Bad:
<div style="background: #FF00001F">

> This mod adds so many skins you can't even imagine!
</div>

Good:
<div style="background: #00FF001F">

> This mod adds custom skins for Crow, Emz, Piper, and Colette, replacing their default skins.
</div>

### Bonus tip

If grammar and formatting are difficult for you, you can ask an LLM for help
(e.g. ChatGPT, DeepSeek). This works well in most cases. But don't overdo it —
AI-generated text can feel off-putting even when it's technically correct.
